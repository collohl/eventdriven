About E-Waste Management System

E-waste, an emerging global environmental issue, is gaining prominence. The rapid growth in Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) contributes to this concern. This trend is expected to persist due to the swift emergence of novel technologies and the availability of inexpensive electrical and electronic products. Consumer electronics evolve rapidly, but limited incentives for designs that promote reduce, reuse, and recycle (3R) lead to frequent obsolescence and disposal. The discarded electronics pose a rising threat due to toxic substances like lead, nickel, cadmium, copper, chromium, beryllium, lithium, and mercury. Mismanagement of e-waste can harm both human health and the environment, given its highly toxic components.
